Temperature Units

CELSIUS

Celsius degrees, also known as Celsius temperature or centigrade, are a metric unit of temperature measurement in which 0°C represents the freezing point and 100°C represents the boiling point of water at standard atmospheric pressure.

FAHRENHEIT

Fahrenheit degrees are a temperature scale commonly used in the United States, where 32°F represents the freezing point and 212°F represents the boiling point of water at standard atmospheric pressure.

Formula

Celsius (°C) to Fahrenheit (°F)

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

This formula involves two steps:

  1. Multiply the Celsius temperature (°C) by 9/5 (which is 1.8).
  2. Add 32 to the result of the multiplication to obtain the equivalent temperature in Fahrenheit (°F).

For example, let's say we have a temperature of 25°C that we want to convert to Fahrenheit:

  • °F = (25°C × 9/5) + 32
  • °F = (45) + 32
  • °F = 77

So, 25°C is equivalent to 77°F.
